# Advanced Data Analytics

---

## Project Overview

This project focuses on applying advanced SQL techniques to transform raw business data into meaningful insights that support decision-making.

Building upon the analytical datasets created in **Project 01 - Data Warehouse** and the foundational exploration performed in **Project 02 - Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)**, this project investigates trends, performance, customer behavior, product performance, and business growth patterns.

The goal is to move beyond basic reporting and leverage SQL as an analytical tool for identifying opportunities, measuring performance, and generating actionable business insights.

## Advanced Analytical Techniques

### 1. Change Over Time Analysis

Analyze how business measures evolve over time.

**Business Value**

* Identify long-term trends
* Detect seasonality patterns
* Monitor growth and decline
* Track business performance over time

**Examples**

* Monthly Sales Trends
* Revenue Growth Trends
* Customer Activity Trends

---

### 2. Cumulative Analysis

Analyze cumulative performance using running totals and window functions.

**Techniques Used**

* Window Functions
* Running Totals
* Cumulative Metrics

**Business Value**

* Understand growth progression
* Track cumulative revenue
* Measure long-term business performance
* Evaluate overall business trajectory

---

### 3. Performance Analysis

Compare current performance against previous periods and benchmark values.

**Techniques Used**

* LAG()
* LEAD()
* Window Functions
* CTEs
* Aggregations

**Business Value**

* Year-over-Year Analysis (YoY)
* Month-over-Month Analysis (MoM)
* Performance Benchmarking
* Variance Analysis

Examples:

* Revenue vs Previous Year
* Monthly Sales Comparison
* Product Performance Evaluation

---

### 4. Part-to-Whole Analysis

Analyze how individual categories contribute to overall business performance.

**Business Value**

* Identify top-performing categories
* Measure contribution percentages
* Understand business composition
* Evaluate category importance

Examples:

* Revenue Contribution by Product Category
* Customer Contribution Analysis
* Market Share Evaluation

---

### 5. Data Segmentation

Group data into meaningful business segments using SQL logic.

**Techniques Used**

* CASE Statements
* Business Rules
* Custom Segmentation Logic

**Business Value**

* Customer Segmentation
* Product Segmentation
* Revenue-Based Segmentation
* Performance Grouping

Examples:

* High Value Customers
* Medium Value Customers
* Low Value Customers
